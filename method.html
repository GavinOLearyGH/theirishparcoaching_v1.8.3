Understand the System

How The Irish Par Caddy turns a scorecard into a coaching plan.

The Caddy does not chase every mistake. It looks for the pattern that mattered most, pulls one clear next lever to the top, and turns that into work across Swing, Skill, Stretch, and Strength.

The TIP Loop

From scorecard to next session.

1
Enter the Round

Scorecard shorthand captures enough signal without turning the process into homework.

2
Read the Pattern

The Caddy studies score, fairways, GIR, putts, penalties, recovery, and approach windows.

3
Find the One Thing

The clearest next lever is pulled to the top so the player does not chase ten fixes.

4
Prescribe the Work

The plan is split across Swing, Skill, Stretch, and Strength.

5
Train and Repeat

Take the next block to practice, then review again after the next round.

The One Thing

Not ten fixes. One useful next action.

Core Principle
The Caddy surfaces the one thing most likely to change your next few rounds.

Golf improvement gets messy when every bad shot becomes a swing rebuild. The Caddy keeps the player focused on the pattern that actually changed the card.

What the Caddy Looks For

The signals behind the debrief.

Penalties and damage control
Three-putts and total putts
Fairway pattern and miss direction
GIR pattern and approach misses
Approach distance buckets
Up-and-down and sand-save chances
Front/back nine balance
Round notes and focus of day
The Four Dimensions

The Caddy builds the player from four sides.

Swing

Mechanics and motion

Contact, face, path, low point, setup, tempo, and delivery.

When the card points to strike or ball-flight pattern, Swing work gives the motion a clearer job.

Skill

Execution and scoring

Putting, wedges, short game, approach windows, tee strategy, and transfer practice.

Skill work turns the pattern into golf tasks instead of range-only fixes.

Stretch

Mobility and freedom

Hips, spine, shoulders, wrists, posture, and movement quality.

Stretch work helps the body access the motion without forcing compensation.

Strength

Stability and support

Core, glutes, balance, rotation control, pressure shift, and force support.

Strength work helps the player hold posture, control speed, and repeat the pattern.

How to Use the Output

Read it in the right order.

1. Read the debrief first
Understand what traveled, what cost shots, and what to train next.
2. Look at the One Thing
This is the highest-priority lever from the round.
3. Review the pattern panels
Use the panels to understand why the Caddy chose that focus.
4. Follow the prescription
Do the work across Swing, Skill, Stretch, and Strength.
5. Do not overreact to one bad shot
The Caddy is built for patterns, not panic.
Studio + Caddy

Build the bag. Build the player.

The Irish Par has two sides. The Studio builds the bag through equipment work, measurement, and fit. The Caddy builds the player through reflection, decision-making, movement, and practice direction.

Equipment matters
